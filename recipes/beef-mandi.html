The Flavor Blueprint

Understanding the foundation of perfect Mandi

The Meat & Broth

We use bone-in beef to create a rich, flavorful broth (*yakhni*). This broth is the liquid gold that will cook our rice, infusing every grain with deep, savory flavor.

üçö

The Rice

Long-grain Basmati is key. Rinsing and soaking removes excess starch, ensuring fluffy, separate grains that perfectly absorb the broth without becoming mushy.

The Smoke (*Tadween*)

The final, magical step. A hot piece of charcoal creates a fragrant smoke that infuses the entire dish with the authentic, unmistakable aroma of traditional Mandi.

Your Secret Weapon: Hawaij Spice Blend

This method provides the most aromatic and potent flavor by toasting whole spices before grinding.

Ingredients:

2 tbsp Cumin Seeds
1.5 tbsp Coriander Seeds
1 tbsp Black Peppercorns
1 tsp Whole Cloves
10-12 Green Cardamom Pods
2 tbsp Ground Turmeric
Instructions:

Toast whole spices (except turmeric) in a dry pan for 2-3 mins until fragrant. Cool, then grind to a fine powder. Mix with turmeric.

This simplified version uses pre-ground spices for maximum convenience and great flavor.

Ingredients:

2 tbsp Cumin Powder
1.5 tbsp Coriander Powder
1 tbsp Black Pepper Powder
1 tsp Ground Cloves
1.5 tsp Cardamom Powder
2 tbsp Turmeric Powder
Instructions:

Combine all ground spices in a small bowl and whisk thoroughly until uniform.

The Cooking Masterclass

Follow these steps to create the perfect Mandi

1

The Pressure Cooker

Build layers of flavor and cook tender beef (~60 min)

This is where the magic begins. We build layers of flavor and cook the beef until it's unbelievably tender, creating our precious broth in the process.

1
Saut√© Aromatics:

Heat ghee in pressure cooker, add whole spices (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, bay leaves) and saut√© for 30 seconds. Add onion and garlic, cook until soft.

2
Sear Beef:

Increase heat, add beef and sear on all sides until well-browned.

3
Quick Marinade:

Reduce heat, stir in ginger-garlic paste and 2 tbsp of your Hawaij blend.

4
Pressure Cook:

Add hot water to cover beef by 1 inch. Add dried lime. Secure lid, bring to pressure, and cook for 45-50 minutes.

5
Separate:

Let pressure release naturally. Remove tender beef pieces. Strain the broth into a separate pot, discarding the whole spices.

2

Cooking the Rice

Use the "liquid gold" broth (~25 min)

Use the "liquid gold" broth to cook the rice. Remember to start this while the beef is finishing its pressure cooking time.

1
Prep Rice:

Rinse Basmati rice until water runs clear. Soak for 30 mins, then drain completely.

2
Measure Broth:

Use a 1.5:1 broth-to-rice ratio (e.g., 3 cups broth for 2 cups rice). Top up with hot water if needed.

3
Cook & Steam:

Bring broth to a rolling boil. Add salt, then rice. Cook until absorbed, then steam on low for 15-20 minutes.

3

The Final Crisp & Smoke

Crispy beef and authentic smoking (~25 min)

The Final Crisp (20 min)

The Smoky Finish (5 min)

Assemble: Place beef on rice in pot

Smoke: Hot charcoal + oil in foil cup

Infuse: Cover tightly for 5-10 minutes
