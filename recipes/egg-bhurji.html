1

Prepare the Eggs

Lightly whisk the eggs in a bowl with a pinch of salt. Don't over-beat; you want streaks of yolk and white visible for the best texture.

üí° Pro Tip: Room temperature eggs scramble more evenly than cold ones.

2

Saut√© the Onions

Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until they turn soft and translucent, about 3-4 minutes.

üí° Pro Tip: Properly cooked onions are the foundation of great bhurji flavor.

3

Add Aromatics

Add the ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Saut√© for another minute until the raw smell disappears and it becomes fragrant.

üí° Pro Tip: This step builds the aromatic base - don't rush it!

4

Cook the Tomatoes

Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy, and oil starts to separate from the mixture. This is crucial for the flavor base.

5

Add the Spices

Add the dry spices: turmeric powder, red chili powder, and pav bhaji masala. Stir well and cook for 30 seconds to bloom the spices.

6

Add the Eggs

Lower the heat and pour in the whisked eggs. Let them sit for about 10-15 seconds before you start scrambling.

üí° Critical: Lower heat prevents the eggs from cooking too quickly!

7

Scramble Gently

Gently scramble the eggs with a spatula. Cook until the eggs are just set but still moist and soft. Avoid overcooking to prevent dry, rubbery bhurji.

8

Final Touches

Turn off the heat. Stir in the chopped fresh coriander. Check for salt and adjust if necessary.

9

Serve & Enjoy!

Serve immediately with hot buttered pav, roti, or toast. Enjoy your delicious homemade egg bhurji!
